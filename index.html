What Qualifies

If It's Not Generating Revenue, It May Be Worth More Than You Think

We evaluate all types of non-revenue-generating property. Here are some of the most common categories that attract serious investor attention.

🌾

Rural Vacant Land

Large rural parcels not in active agricultural production — flat land with nearby highway or rail access is particularly sought after.

🏙️

Urban Infill & Brownfields

City lots, former industrial sites, and infill parcels in or near employment centers. North St. Louis and similar urban core areas are of strong interest.

✈️

Airport-Adjacent Properties

Properties near major and regional airports benefit from existing power infrastructure, fiber connectivity, and transportation access — prime data center criteria.

🏚️

Dilapidated & Condemned

Structures that are partially or fully condemned, derelict, or scheduled for demolition. Investors often prefer acquiring and clearing rather than paying a premium for a clean site.

🏭

Former Industrial & Commercial

Old warehouses, distribution centers, manufacturing plants, and retail boxes. These often have the power capacity and floor loading that data centers require.

📦

Opportunity Zone Parcels

Properties located within designated Opportunity Zones carry significant tax advantages for investors, dramatically increasing buyer interest and potential valuations.
